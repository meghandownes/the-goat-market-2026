Semester Project: Let‚Äôs Play Policy: Who‚Äôs The GOAT?

Total Points: 200 (Policy Brief: 100 | Presentation: 75 | Peer Evaluation: 25)
Due Date: Week 15 (May 7, 2026)
Group Size: 2-3 students

Project Overview

This capstone project asks you to develop and defend a policy recommendation for addressing a major macroeconomic challenge. You‚Äôll research different policy approaches, analyze them through the lens of economic theories you‚Äôve learned, and present evidence-based arguments for why your recommended approach is the ‚ÄúGreatest Approach of All Time‚Äù (GOAT).

Part A: Policy Brief (100 points)

A comprehensive written analysis (2,000-2,500 words) including:

1. Executive Summary (150-200 words)

  • Clear statement of the macroeconomic issue
  • Why it matters
  • Your recommended policy approach
  • Expected outcomes

2. Issue Definition & Background (400-500 words)

Cover: - What is the specific economic problem? - Historical context: How did we get here? - Current data and trends - Who is affected and how? - Why does this problem matter?

Include: - At least 2-3 relevant data sources/graphs - Key statistics and figures - Timeline of the problem if relevant

3. Theoretical Analysis (600-700 words)

Analyze the problem through multiple economic lenses:

Theory 1: [e.g., Keynesian Economics] - How does this theory explain the problem? - What policies does this theory suggest? - Strengths of this approach - Limitations of this approach

Theory 2: [e.g., Monetarism] - How does this theory explain the problem? - What policies does this theory suggest? - Strengths of this approach - Limitations of this approach

Theory 3: [e.g., Supply-Side/Growth Economics] - How does this theory explain the problem? - What policies does this theory suggest? - Strengths of this approach - Limitations of this approach

4. Policy Options Analysis (600-700 words)

Examine at least 3 different policy approaches:

Option A: [Policy Name] - Description of the policy - How it would work - Pros: - [Advantage 1] - [Advantage 2] - Cons: - [Disadvantage 1] - [Disadvantage 2] - Real-world examples (case studies, countries, time periods) - Estimated costs/benefits

Option B: [Policy Name] - [Same structure as Option A]

Option C: [Policy Name] - [Same structure as Option A]

Optional: Option D or E if comparing many approaches

5. Your Recommendation & Justification (400-500 words)

This is your GOAT argument:

  • Which policy do you recommend and why?
  • Why is this the ‚ÄúGreatest Approach of All Time‚Äù for this issue?
  • How does it address the root causes identified in your analysis?
  • What does economic theory predict about its effectiveness?
  • What evidence supports your choice?

Address: - Why you rejected the other options - Potential criticisms of your approach and how you‚Äôd respond - How this policy compares to historical attempts - Expected outcomes and timeline

6. Implementation Considerations (200-300 words)

  • Who would implement this policy?
  • Timeline for implementation
  • Potential obstacles
  • How to measure success
  • Transition costs or disruptions
  • Political feasibility

7. References (5-10 sources minimum)

Include: - Academic articles - Government reports/data - News sources - Books - Reputable policy organizations

Format: APA or Chicago style (consistent throughout)

Part B: Presentation (75 points)

A 15-20 minute group presentation covering your policy analysis and recommendation.

Slide Deck Components

  1. Title Slide (1 slide)
    • Topic/Issue
    • Group members
    • Date
  2. Problem Definition (2-3 slides)
    • What‚Äôs the issue?
    • Why does it matter?
    • Key data/statistics with visuals
  3. Theoretical Analysis (3-4 slides)
    • How different theories explain the problem
    • Visual representation (maybe a table)
    • Quote from or reference to economic theory
  4. Policy Options (4-5 slides)
    • Overview of options examined
    • Pros/cons comparison (table or visual)
    • Real-world examples
  5. Your Recommendation (2-3 slides)
    • Your GOAT policy approach
    • Why you chose this
    • Expected impacts
  6. Implementation & Conclusion (1-2 slides)
    • How it works
    • Next steps
    • Key takeaway

Presentation Tips

  • Visuals: Use charts, graphs, and images
  • Data: Include specific numbers and trends
  • Clarity: Explain concepts in accessible language
  • Engagement: Tell stories, use examples
  • Timing: Practice to fit in 15-20 minutes
  • Delivery: Speak clearly, maintain eye contact, show enthusiasm

Grading Criteria (75 points)

Criterion Points
Content Clarity 25
Visual Design & Organization 20
Presentation Delivery 20
Q&A / Audience Engagement 10

Part C: Peer Evaluation (25 points)

Each group member evaluates the contributions of their peers.

What to Evaluate: - Who did what portion of the project? - How fairly was work distributed? - Did everyone contribute meaningfully? - Quality of individual contributions

Individual Score Adjustment: - If all members contributed equally: Everyone gets the full 25 points - If unequal contributions: Individual scores adjusted (e.g., 28 points for strong contributor, 22 points for weaker contributor)

  • Is productivity growth slowing? What to do about it?
  • Technology adoption and economic impact
  • Research and development incentives

  • What‚Äôs the optimal tax rate or tax structure?
  • How much should government spend?

  • Progressive taxation and redistribution
  • Wealth taxes vs. income taxes

International Economics

Financial System

Climate & Sustainability

Format Requirements

Policy Brief

  • File format: .qmd (Quarto markdown)
  • Length: 2,000-2,500 words (excluding references)
  • Font: 12pt, standard font (Arial, Times New Roman, Calibri)
  • Spacing: Double-spaced or 1.5-spaced
  • Headings: Clear hierarchy with ### and #### for subheadings
  • Include: Table of contents, page numbers, citations

Presentation

  • Format: PDF slides or Quarto HTML presentation
  • Length: 15-20 minutes
  • Minimum 10 slides, maximum 20 slides
  • Include: Data visualizations, images, clear text
  • Accessible: Large enough text, good color contrast
